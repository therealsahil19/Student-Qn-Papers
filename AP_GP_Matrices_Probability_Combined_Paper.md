# SECTION A — Multiple Choice Questions [10 × 1 = 10 Marks]

**1.** The 7th term of the A.P. $\frac{1}{a}, \left( \frac{1}{a} + 1 \right), \left( \frac{1}{a} + 2 \right), \dots$ is:
   - (a) $\left( \frac{1}{a} + 6 \right)$
   - (b) $\left( \frac{1}{a} + 7 \right)$
   - (c) $\left( \frac{1}{a} + 8 \right)$
   - (d) $\left( \frac{1}{a} + 7^2 \right)$

**2.** The 15th term from the end of the A.P. $3, 8, 13, \dots, 253$ is:
   - (a) 183
   - (b) 188
   - (c) 193
   - (d) 178

**3.** Consider the following two sequences:
   - A: $0.003, 0.003\sqrt{10}, 0.03$
   - B: $4\sqrt{2}, -4, 2\sqrt{2}$

   Which of the following holds true?
   - (a) Only A represents a G.P.
   - (b) Both A and B represent G.P.
   - (c) Only B represents a G.P.
   - (d) Both A and B do not represent a G.P.

**4.** The sum of first $n$ terms of the series $a, 3a, 5a, \dots$ is:
   - (a) $n^2 a^2$
   - (b) $n^2 a$
   - (c) $\frac{(2n-1)an}{2}$
   - (d) $na$

**5.** If $A = [a_{ij}]_{2 \times 2}$ where $a_{ij} = i + j$, then $A$ is equal to:
   - (a) $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix}$
   - (b) $\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix}$
   - (c) $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$
   - (d) $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$

**6.** If $A = \begin{pmatrix} x & 3 \\ y & 3 \end{pmatrix}$, $I$ is an identity matrix of order 2 and $A^2 = 3I$, then:
   - (a) $x = -3, y = -2$
   - (b) $x = 3, y = 2$
   - (c) $x = 3, y = -2$
   - (d) $x = -3, y = 2$

**7.** A die is rolled once. The probability of getting a number not divisible by 2 or 3 is:
   - (a) $\frac{2}{3}$
   - (b) $\frac{5}{6}$
   - (c) $\frac{1}{6}$
   - (d) $\frac{1}{3}$

**8.** Three identical coins are tossed together. The probability of obtaining exactly two heads is:
   - (a) $\frac{3}{8}$
   - (b) $\frac{1}{8}$
   - (c) $\frac{7}{8}$
   - (d) $\frac{1}{4}$

**9.** **Assertion (A):** If $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 2 & Z \end{pmatrix} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$, then the order of matrix $Z$ is $1 \times 1$.
    **Reason (R):** The product of 2 matrices $AB$ is possible if the number of columns in $A$ is equal to the number of rows in $B$.
   - (a) Both A and R are correct and R is the correct explanation for A.
   - (b) Both A and R are correct but R is not the correct explanation for A.
   - (c) A is correct but R is not correct.
   - (d) A is incorrect but R is correct.

**10.** **Assertion (A):** A die is thrown once and the probability of getting an even number is $\frac{2}{3}$.
      **Reason (R):** The sample space for even numbers on a die is $\{2, 4, 6\}$.
   - (a) A is true, R is false.
   - (b) A is false, R is true.
   - (c) Both A and R are true.
   - (d) Both A and R are false.

# SECTION B — Short Answer Questions [6 × 2 + 6 × 3 = 30 Marks]

## 2 Marks Questions

**11.** If $(k - 3), (2k + 1),$ and $(4k + 3)$ are three consecutive terms of an A.P., find the value of $k$.

**12.** The 7th term and the 9th term of a geometric progression are $\frac{128}{625}$ and $\frac{512}{15625}$ respectively. Find the common ratio.

**13.** If the third term of a G.P. is 4, determine the product of its first five terms.

**14.** If $\begin{pmatrix} x+3 & 4 \\ y-4 & x+y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & 4 \\ 3 & 9 \end{pmatrix}$, find the values of $x$ and $y$.

**15.** What is the probability that a leap year, selected at random, will have 53 Fridays?

**16.** A game of numbers has cards marked with $11, 12, 13, \dots, 40$. A card is drawn at random. Find the probability that the number on the card is a perfect square.

## 3 Marks Questions

**17.** In an Arithmetic Progression, the 6th term is equal to four times its first term and the sum of first six terms is 75. Find the first term and the common difference.

**18.** Write the $n$th term ($T_n$) of an Arithmetic Progression consisting of all whole numbers which are divisible by 3 and 7. Find the sum of first 10 terms of this A.P.

**19.** The first term of a G.P. is $-3$ and the square of the second term is equal to its 4th term. Find its 7th term.

**20.** Simplify:

$$\sin A \begin{pmatrix} \sin A & -\cos A \\ \cos A & \sin A \end{pmatrix} + \cos A \begin{pmatrix} \cos A & \sin A \\ -\sin A & \cos A \end{pmatrix}$$

**21.** Given $A = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 7 \\ 2 & 4 \end{pmatrix}, B = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 2 \\ 5 & 3 \end{pmatrix}, C = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -5 \\ -4 & 6 \end{pmatrix}$ and $AB + 2X = C^2 I$. Find the matrix $X$ where $I$ is the identity matrix of order 2.

**22.** A bag contains $x$ red balls, $(x + 5)$ blue balls and $(3x + 10)$ white balls. If the probability of drawing a blue ball is $\frac{2}{9}$, find the value of $x$. Hence find the probability of drawing a white ball.
