SECTION I  (10 × 1 = 10 Marks)
(Multiple Choice Questions – Select the correct option)

1.  The number of sides of a regular polygon in which each interior angle exceeds each exterior angle by 132° is:
    (a) 8    (b) 10    (c) 12    (d) 15

2.  In a convex polygon, the number of diagonals is twice the number of sides. The polygon is a:
    (a) Heptagon    (b) Octagon    (c) Nonagon    (d) Decagon

3.  Which set of data does NOT guarantee a unique construction of quadrilateral ABCD?
    (a) Four sides and one diagonal
    (b) Three sides and two included angles  
    (c) Two adjacent sides and three angles
    (d) Four sides and one angle

4.  In rhombus PQRS, diagonal PR subtends an angle of 50° at side PS. The angle between the diagonals is:
    (a) 40°    (b) 50°    (c) 65°    (d) 80°

5.  The sum of the interior angles at the five vertices of a pentagram (five-pointed star) is equal to:
    (a) 180°    (b) 360°    (c) 540°    (d) 720°

6.  In parallelogram ABCD, a line through A cuts diagonal BD at P, BC at Q and DC produced at R. If BP:PD = 1:2, then AQ:QR equals:
    (a) 1:1    (b) 1:2    (c) 2:1    (d) 3:2

7.  A convex polygon has interior angles that form an arithmetic progression: 120°, 125°, 130°, ... The number of sides is:
    (a) 7    (b) 9    (c) 11    (d) 13

8.  In rectangle ABCD, diagonals AC and BD intersect at O. If ∠OAB = 35°, then ∠BOC equals:
    (a) 70°    (b) 110°    (c) 145°    (d) 35°

9.  Which condition is both necessary and sufficient to prove that a parallelogram is a square?
    (a) Diagonals are equal
    (b) Diagonals bisect each other at 90°
    (c) Diagonals are equal and bisect at 90°
    (d) One angle is 90°

10. The exterior angles of a polygon are in the ratio 1:2:3:4:5:6. The largest interior angle measures:
    (a) 120°    (b) 140°    (c) 160°    (d) 170°


SECTION II  (30 Marks)

Part A  (6 × 2 = 12 Marks)
(Short Answer Questions)

11. The interior angle of a regular polygon is eight times the exterior angle. Determine the number of sides and the total number of diagonals.

12. Outline the first three steps only for constructing quadrilateral ABCD where AB = 5 cm, BC = 6 cm, CD = 4.8 cm, ∠ABC = 75° and ∠BCD = 105°.

13. In quadrilateral ABCD, the angles are in the ratio ∠A:∠B:∠C:∠D = 2:3:5:6. Calculate the measure of the largest angle.

14. Justify, using the triangle inequality theorem, why a quadrilateral with sides AB = 3 cm, BC = 4 cm, CD = 5 cm and diagonal AC = 9 cm cannot be constructed.

15. In parallelogram ABCD, the bisectors of consecutive angles ∠A and ∠B intersect at P. Prove that ∠APB = 90° without using the angle sum property of triangles.

16. A hexagon has four right angles and two equal obtuse angles. Calculate the measure of each obtuse angle.


Part B  (6 × 3 = 18 Marks)
(Medium Answer Questions)

17. ABCDE is a regular pentagon. Diagonal AD intersects diagonal BE at point P.
    (a) Prove that ∠EAP = 36°.
    (b) Hence, calculate the measure of ∠APB.

18. Construct quadrilateral WXYZ in which WX = 6.2 cm, XY = 5.8 cm, YZ = 6 cm, ∠WXY = 85° and ∠XYZ = 95°. Write the steps of construction in sequence.

19. In the given figure (not drawn to scale), ABCD is a rhombus with ∠BAD = 70°. Diagonal BD is produced to E such that BD = DE. Join CE.
    (a) Find ∠DBC.
    (b) Prove that ΔBCE is isosceles.
    (c) Calculate ∠BCE.

20. Each interior angle of a polygon is 140° greater than each exterior angle. Find:
    (a) The number of sides of the polygon.
    (b) The number of diagonals from one vertex.
    (c) The total number of diagonals.

21. In parallelogram PQRS, the bisector of ∠P intersects QR at M and SR produced at N.
    (a) Prove that ΔPQM is isosceles.
    (b) Prove that Q is the mid-point of RN, given that PS = 2PQ.

22. A seven-pointed star (heptagram) has vertices A, B, C, D, E, F, G arranged alternately on a circle.
    (a) Calculate the sum of angles at the seven points: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C + ∠D + ∠E + ∠F + ∠G.
    (b) If the star is regular, find the measure of each point angle.


SECTION III  (4 × 10 = 40 Marks)
(Long Answer Questions)

23. (a) Construct quadrilateral ABCD with the following data: [4]
         AB = 4.8 cm, BC = 5.4 cm, CD = 6 cm, ∠ABC = 80°, ∠BCD = 100°.
         Draw diagonal AC and measure its length.
         
    (b) Using the cosine rule (or otherwise), verify theoretically whether the measured length of AC in part (a) is consistent with the given data. Show your working. [3]
    
    (c) State and prove that the line segment joining the mid-points of any two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third side and half of it. [3]

24. (a) In the figure below (not shown to scale), ABCD and ABEF are parallelograms on the same base AB but on opposite sides. The line DE intersects AB at P and BC at Q. Given that ∠DAB = 60° and diagonal AC makes an angle of 40° with AB, calculate: [5]
         (i)   ∠APD
         (ii)  ∠CQB
         (iii) All angles of quadrilateral CEPQ, where E is the vertex of parallelogram ABEF opposite to B.
         
    (b) Two regular polygons have their number of sides in the ratio 1:2. The ratio of their interior angles is 3:4. Find the number of sides of each polygon and justify your solution with the polygon angle-sum formula. [5]

25. (a) Construct a rhombus ABCD in which diagonal AC = 10 cm and side AB = 6 cm. Measure and record the length of diagonal BD. [5]
    
    (b) In the rhombus constructed above, prove using the Pythagorean theorem that your measurement of BD is theoretically correct. [2]
    
    (c) Prove that in any quadrilateral, if the diagonals bisect each other at right angles, then it is a rhombus. [3]

26. (a) A hexagonal pavement tile has three consecutive angles each measuring 140°. The remaining three angles are equal to each other but not equal to 140°. [4]
         (i)  Find the measure of each of the remaining three angles.
         (ii) Determine whether this hexagon can be cyclic (all vertices lying on a circle). Justify your answer using angle properties.
         
    (b) In convex quadrilateral ABCD, the internal bisectors of ∠A and ∠C intersect at P, while the internal bisectors of ∠B and ∠D intersect at Q. [3]
         (i)  Prove that ∠APC + ∠BQD = 180°.
         (ii) If ABCD is a parallelogram, prove that P and Q coincide.
         
    (c) A mason constructs a quadrilateral concrete slab with sides AB = 8 m, BC = 6 m, CD = 8 m, DA = 6 m, and one diagonal AC = 10 m. Explain three distinct methods he can use to verify (without measuring angles) that the slab is a rectangle. [3]
